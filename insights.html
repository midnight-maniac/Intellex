Q

What are the key differences between BERT and the original Transformer?

10 Feb 2026

The original Transformer (Vaswani et al.) is an encoder-decoder model designed for sequence-to-sequence tasks like translation. BERT uses only the encoder stack and is trained with a masked language modeling objective, making it bidirectional by design. BERT is optimized for transfer learning across classification and extraction tasks, while the original Transformer is end-to-end for generation tasks.

Q

Summarize the practical implications of neural scaling laws for my research.

8 Feb 2026

Kaplan et al.'s findings suggest that for a fixed compute budget, training a larger model on proportionally fewer tokens is more efficient than training a smaller model to convergence. This has direct implications for resource planning: if you are fine-tuning or working with limited compute, it may be more effective to start from a larger pre-trained base than to train a smaller model longer. The revised Chinchilla scaling laws further tighten these compute-optimal recommendations.
