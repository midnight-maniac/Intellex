Attention Is All You Need

Vaswani et al. • Summarized 12 Feb 2026
▼

TL;DR: Introduces the Transformer architecture, replacing recurrence with self-attention for sequence transduction tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results in translation.

  • Proposes multi-head self-attention as the core mechanism
  • Eliminates recurrent and convolutional layers entirely
  • Achieves 28.4 BLEU on WMT 2014 English-to-German translation
  • Trains significantly faster due to parallelization

BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers

Devlin et al. • Summarized 10 Feb 2026
▼

TL;DR: BERT introduces bidirectional pre-training for language models using masked language modeling and next sentence prediction, enabling fine-tuning on a wide range of NLP tasks.

  • Bidirectional context — unlike GPT which is left-to-right only
  • Pre-trained on BooksCorpus and English Wikipedia
  • Sets new SOTA on 11 NLP benchmarks including GLUE and SQuAD
  • Fine-tuning requires minimal task-specific architecture changes

Research Notes - Neural Scaling Laws

Kaplan et al. • Summarized 8 Feb 2026
▼

TL;DR: Empirically demonstrates that language model performance follows predictable power-law scaling with compute, data, and parameter count.

  • Loss scales as a power law with model size, dataset size, and compute
  • Optimal allocation: larger models trained on fewer tokens outperform smaller models
  • Diminishing returns from increasing only one factor
  • Chinchilla later revised these compute-optimal ratios
